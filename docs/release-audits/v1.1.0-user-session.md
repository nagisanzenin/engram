## User session report — v1.1.0

topic: Vietnamese tone-mark placement (which vowel letter carries the dấu)
mode: standard ×2 + deep ×1 + quick review ×1 (4 sittings, time-travelled 07-22 → 07-24)
real minutes: ~70 encoding + 4 review     nodes encoded: 10 of 14     reviews cleared: 3

I picked this because I genuinely place Vietnamese tone marks by feel and get vowel
clusters wrong. I did not know the answer to `mark-anchors-nucleus` before the session and
said so in the pretest ("i just know the rule of thumb… the principle behind it i cannot
state"). That was true, not performed.

### WHAT WORKED

- **The pretest immediately paid for itself.** One of three probes came back solid and got
  scheduled 14 days out instead of being taught; two came back empty and stayed `new`. No
  time was spent teaching me something I already had.
- **The concept half is unchanged and still good.** Threshold nodes (`vowel-letter-roles`,
  `mark-anchors-nucleus`) ran the ordinary grammar; the derivation ("mark = the nucleus's
  letter") actually reorganized the rules I had been memorizing as a list. That is the
  threshold-concept claim doing real work.
- **The blind assessor caught me.** I thought `qu-gi-onsets` was a clean answer. It graded
  **partial**, correctly: the rubric wanted the canonical misplacements the rule kills
  (qúa, qủa, gía) and I never named them. Rating `hard`, due in one day instead of two
  weeks. That is the separation of powers earning its keep — the tutor (me) was satisfied
  and the grader was not.
- **The procedure ladder felt different from the concept beats, in the right way.** Rung
  selection put me at L1 because my signals were ambiguous ("shaky" + no pretest), and the
  worked example → completion → faded → cold-solve progression on syllable parsing is a
  genuinely different activity from explain-it-back. The faded rung blanked the
  principle-bearing step (glide-vs-nucleus after qu-), which is where I actually needed to
  think.
- **The fresh-instance rule is the part I'd defend hardest.** VERIFY served me `tuyệt`, not
  the canonical `khuỷu` whose full parse I'd just watched. Had it re-served `khuỷu` I would
  have "passed" by recall and learned nothing. Computing the answer key by execution before
  serving caught nothing this time — but it cost seconds and the failure it prevents (a
  wrong key = a false lapse on my schedule) is exactly the kind I could not detect myself.
- **The procedure grade landed correctly, and sharper than I expected.** My `tuyệt` parse
  guessed at the `y` by ear and said so. The blind assessor returned **partial / hard /
  `error_class: conceptual`** — correctly refusing to call a by-ear guess a slip (a slip is
  method-right; I had a method gap) — and it independently noticed something I hadn't:
  *"this instance carries no digraph, so qu-/gi-/ngh- absorption is untested by it."* It
  audited my fresh-variant choice, not just my answer.
- **Every number was honest, including the ones that made the session look thin** (below).

### WHAT CONFUSED ME

- After the pretest I wasn't sure whether the two *missed* probes had been recorded or
  discarded. They stay `new` by design, and the skill says so — but as a learner mid-session
  I couldn't tell, and I had to run `topic-status` to reassure myself.
- The topic came back with 14 nodes and I encoded 10. There is no "you are 10/14 through the
  arc" line in the close; `topic-status` has the bar but you have to ask for it.

### WHAT ANNOYED ME — and what I would have quit over

- **Nothing in the procedure path.** But: the architect took ~7 minutes of silence to build
  the map, and the "this is the one slow step" warning in `/learn` is the only thing that
  made it survivable. That warning is load-bearing; had it not been there I would have
  assumed the tool had hung.
- Minor: three procedure-shaped nodes (`parse-syllable-roles`, `place-the-mark`) sit late in
  the order behind seven concepts. That is correct pedagogically (they *require* the
  concepts) and still meant an hour before I touched the thing I actually came for —
  placing marks. I would not change the DAG; I would consider saying so out loud at intake.

The engine never once told me something flattering that wasn't true. The closest it came to
a temptation — a 100% concept arm at n=3 — is precisely where it stayed quiet.

### WOULD A STRANGER GET THROUGH THIS?

**Yes**, with one caveat I'd want on the record: the procedure path only appears if the
architect declares a procedure node, and for a topic that is *all* concepts a stranger will
never see any of this release. That is the design (Engram stays general; the content decides)
— but it means v1.1's value is invisible on many topics, and the CHANGELOG should not oversell
it. It doesn't.
